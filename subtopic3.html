Abstract

Preventing computer viruses is possible when users pay attention to what they click and open online. One of the easiest ways to avoid viruses is to stay away from suspicious links, unsafe downloads, and unknown email attachments. Keeping devices updated to the latest version is also important because updates often fix security issues. Antivirus software can add another layer of protection by helping detect and remove threats before they cause more damage. Overall, being aware and practicing safe online habits can make a big difference in protecting computers and other devices from viruses and malware infections.

Abstract

A big part of preventing viruses is knowing how attackers try to trick people. Methods like phishing and social engineering are used to get users to click suspicious links or share personal information. These attacks usually look like normal emails or messages, which is why they are easy to fall for. It is important to check where messages are coming from and avoid clicking on anything that could be unsafe. Being careful online and staying aware of suspicious messages can help lower the risk of falling for phishing attacks and can also help prevent computer viruses from spreading.
