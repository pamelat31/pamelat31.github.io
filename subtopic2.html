Abstract

Computer viruses spread by attaching themselves to files and programs and then moving from one device to another. This commonly happens through email attachments, especially when someone opens a file that is already infected but does not realize it. Computer viruses can also spread when users open suspicious links or download unsafe files. It is common for people to accidentally spread viruses just by clicking infected content. Since viruses often depend on user actions to spread, it is important to be aware of what attachments, downloads, and websites you click on in order to avoid getting computer viruses on your device.

Site AuthorKaspersky
Abstract

Viruses spread by infecting files and then copying themselves to other systems in many different ways. This can happen through downloads from suspicious websites, email attachments, and shared files that may not seem dangerous at first. Once a virus infects a file, it can continue to spread by attaching itself to another file or program. Viruses are hard to control and dangerous because they can replicate quickly and in an undercover way, so the user may not notice the infection right away. This is why users need to be careful when downloading files, opening attachments, and using unfamiliar websites.
